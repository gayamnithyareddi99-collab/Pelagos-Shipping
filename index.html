Core Platform Suites

Four Suites. One Complete Maritime Execution Intelligence Platform.

PELAGOS SHIPPING combines cargo execution intelligence, trade workflow automation, vessel and voyage management, port disruption visibility, AI-assisted document intelligence, charter-party risk scanning, predictive demurrage alerts, and workflow-native compliance into one unified SaaS platform built for UK maritime brokers, freight forwarders, NVOCCs, ship owners, vessel operators, and logistics SMEs.

Suite 01

Cargo Suite

Manage customer enquiries, quote preparation, cargo records, shipment and route information, document upload and extraction, discrepancy detection, customer communication, task ownership, and operational alerts in one connected workspace.

The Cargo Suite connects enquiry management, quote history, cargo records, document intelligence, customer communication, and compliance checks into one shipment execution workspace. The CoastBorder module provides post-Brexit trade compliance support helping UK freight forwarders, customs agents, importers, and exporters organise trade-compliance checks around UK Global Tariff rules, rules of origin, VAT evidence, controlled goods checks, and Northern Ireland / Windsor Framework considerations. Every cargo record links documents, compliance status, tasks, customer updates, and operational milestones into one connected execution environment.
Suite 02

Trade Suite

Connect commercial enquiries, quote history, customer and counterparty records, freight pricing notes, charter-party risk review, sanctions workflow, trade-lane intelligence, customer communication, and commercial memory in one trade execution workspace.

The Trade Suite supports maritime brokers, NVOCCs, traders, charterers, and commercial teams. CoastArb provides London arbitration intelligence linking charter-party clauses, voyage events, demurrage exposure, documents, communications, and claim deadlines into one dispute-risk workflow. CoastEdge UK connects Baltic Exchange market indicators, FFA signals, quote history, and negotiation records so brokers make better-informed pricing decisions. Every commercial quote, fixture, counterparty record, and market signal connects through the Maritime Execution Graph.
Suite 03

Vessel Suite

Connect vessel records, voyage records, route details, port calls, operational events, charter-party terms, laytime and demurrage exposure, document readiness, customer update requirements, and vessel-related compliance checks into one voyage intelligence workspace.

The Vessel Suite treats vessel data as an input into execution, not as a standalone tracking product. The Event-to-Decision Engine converts vessel delays, port congestion, ETA changes, and Notice of Readiness status into demurrage alerts, document checks, customer updates, and escalation tasks. CoastGreen EU/UK supports EU ETS, FuelEU Maritime, carbon-cost allocation, emissions evidence, and charter-party responsibility for environmental costs. Every vessel event connects to cargo, contract, customer, compliance, and financial exposure in real time.
Suite 04

Port & Agent Workflow

Manage port call information, local agent tasks, port documentation, berth and waiting-time updates, customs or terminal disruption notes, port-related customer updates, local operational alerts, and port-level audit trails.

The Port and Agent Workflow suite connects port disruption intelligence directly to live cargo, vessel, voyage, document, customer, and demurrage records. UK Port Pulse captures and organises port disruption signals from users, agents, operators, and available data sources covering berth congestion, customs queues, Ro-Ro disruption, ferry disruption, terminal bottlenecks, documentation delays, and feeder issues. Port disruption is only useful when it connects to the live workflow so PELAGOS doesn't just display it. It identifies which shipments or voyages may be affected and creates follow-up tasks and customer-update prompts automatically.
Feature 05

AI Document Intelligence Workspace

Extract, validate, and compare bills of lading, commercial invoices, packing lists, charter parties, Notices of Readiness, Statements of Facts, and customs records. Flag discrepancies, create review tasks, and link documents to live shipment workflows automatically.

The Document Intelligence Workspace extracts key fields from maritime documents shipper, consignee, vessel, voyage, port of loading, port of discharge, cargo description, weight, quantity, invoice value, container number, and document date. It then compares documents against cargo, voyage, quote, customer, and contract records to identify inconsistencies. Example: invoice shows "Mumbai to Felixstowe" but bill of lading shows "Nhava Sheva to Southampton" PELAGOS flags the mismatch, creates a task, and prevents final document approval until reviewed. Every document is linked to live execution state.
Feature 06

Charter-Party Risk Scanner & Demurrage Alerts

Scan charter-party documents for laytime clauses, demurrage rates, notice obligations, sanctions wording, arbitration clauses, liability shifts, and unusual provisions. Predict demurrage exposure before it becomes a claim using ETA movement, laytime, port conditions, and document readiness.

The Charter-Party Risk Scanner reviews uploaded charter-party documents and converts clause language into workflow logic. A notice clause becomes a workflow deadline. A sanctions clause triggers a screening workflow. A demurrage clause feeds an exposure model. An arbitration clause becomes a review flag. The Predictive Demurrage Prevention Layer links ETA changes, laytime allowance, port delay, cargo readiness, document readiness, NOR status, SOF data, and historical waiting patterns to predict demurrage risk before it occurs generating warnings, assigning tasks, and prompting customer communication before exposure becomes a claim.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Need to Know About PELAGOS SHIPPING

What is the "Maritime Execution Gap" that PELAGOS addresses?

+
The "Maritime Execution Gap" is the critical operational void between when maritime information becomes available and when a team knows what to do with it. Most existing systems vessel trackers, freight marketplaces, document folders, and generic CRMs provide visibility but not action. A vessel delay shows on a dashboard, but no system automatically checks the customer commitment, laytime exposure, missing documents, demurrage risk, and required customer update. PELAGOS bridges this gap by connecting maritime events to practical workflow actions through the Maritime Execution Graph and Event-to-Decision Engine.

How does PELAGOS differ from vessel-tracking tools and maritime data platforms?

+
Vessel-tracking tools show where a vessel is. PELAGOS answers what that means. If a vessel is delayed, existing tracking platforms display the new ETA. PELAGOS links the delay to the customer's commitment, the charter-party laytime terms, the document readiness status, the demurrage exposure calculation, the outstanding task owners, and the required customer update. It then creates tasks, drafts a customer communication, flags missing documents, and triggers a risk review all connected through the Maritime Execution Graph. PELAGOS is a workflow execution system, not a data visualisation product.

What is the Maritime Execution Graph and why is it important?

+
The Maritime Execution Graph is the connected data model that links every aspect of a maritime job customer, enquiry, quote, cargo, route, vessel, voyage, documents, charter-party clauses, compliance checks, port events, demurrage exposure, tasks, communications, and final outcomes into one structured execution environment. This is more powerful than a database because it models the commercial life of a maritime job as a connected decision system. When any element changes a vessel delays, a document is flagged, a sanctions concern arises the graph connects the impact to every related record and generates workflow actions automatically.

How does the Predictive Demurrage Prevention feature work?

+
The Predictive Demurrage Prevention Layer combines multiple operational signals: vessel ETA changes, laytime allowance, port delay data, cargo readiness status, document readiness, NOR (Notice of Readiness) status, SOF (Statement of Facts) data, historical waiting patterns at specific ports, and customer commitments. When these signals combine to suggest demurrage exposure is increasing, PELAGOS generates a warning before the laytime is consumed assigning a task, prompting the user to communicate with the counterparty or customer, and flagging missing documents or required actions while there is still time to prevent the claim.

Does the Charter-Party Scanner replace maritime legal advice?

+
No. The Charter-Party Risk Scanner is positioned as a first-layer commercial risk assistant, not legal advice. It flags unusual clauses, missing terms, deadlines, and risks that require human review but does not replace maritime legal professionals. If a high-risk clause is identified, the platform creates an escalation task and recommends professional legal review. PELAGOS will not automate legal, compliance, customs, or arbitration decisions. The platform helps users identify, organise, explain, and escalate risk but final decisions remain with the user, qualified professionals, or authorised compliance personnel.

How does the Sanctions and Counterparty Workflow operate?

+
The Sanctions and Counterparty Workflow embeds compliance checks directly into the live quote, booking, cargo, vessel, and trade execution workflow rather than treating sanctions as a separate manual search. When a counterparty, vessel, route, or cargo creates a concern, the platform pauses the relevant workflow, assigns a review task, requests missing ownership information, and records all user actions in a full audit trail. This is OFSI-compatible compliance support not automatic sanctions clearance. PELAGOS helps users identify, review, escalate, and document sanctions concerns in a structured way that demonstrates compliance governance.

What are the UK-specific modules in PELAGOS and why are they different?

+
PELAGOS includes six UK-specific intelligence modules: CoastBorder (post-Brexit trade compliance for customs-readiness checks, UK Global Tariff rules, and rules-of-origin workflow); CoastArb (London arbitration intelligence for charter-party dispute risk, demurrage claim preparation, and time-bar tracking); UK Port Pulse (UK port disruption visibility for berth congestion, customs queues, Ro-Ro disruption, and ferry delays); CoastGuard UK (OFSI sanctions workflow with escalation records and audit trails); CoastEdge UK (Baltic/FFA freight intelligence for broker negotiation and market context); and CoastGreen EU/UK (EU ETS and FuelEU Maritime carbon-compliance workflow). These modules are embedded inside the Maritime Execution Graph not separate standalone tools.
